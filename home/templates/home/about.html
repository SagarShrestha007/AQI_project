About AQIntelX

AQIntelX stands for Air Quality Intelligence eXperience. It is a final-year project that integrates real-time air quality monitoring, machine learning-based prediction models, and a user-friendly web interface to deliver accurate and actionable insights about air pollution.

Data Source

The system uses hardware sensors that continuously measure key environmental parameters, including PM2.5, PM10, pressure, carbon monoxide (CO), temperature, and humidity. This data is uploaded in real time to cloud storage and accessed by the system to ensure up-to-date readings.

The collected data is used to compute the current Air Quality Index (AQI), with a focus on PM2.5 and PM10, which are the most significant pollutants affecting air quality.

Machine Learning Models

AQIntelX utilizes two CatBoost regression models, each trained for a specific location: Kathmandu and Changunarayan. When users select a location, the corresponding model predicts the AQI for that area.

For model training, historical air quality and weather data were gathered from three APIs: PurpleAir, OpenAQ, and OpenWeather. These diverse datasets provided a strong foundation to train and validate the models effectively.

Predictions are presented through interactive graphs and clear explanations, making the information accessible and easy to interpret.

Additional Insights

Although multiple environmental parameters are measured, AQI calculations and predictions emphasize PM2.5 and PM10 due to their significant health impacts.

The website also includes educational content that explains how each parameter relates to air quality and health, helping users understand the importance of these readings.
